Introduction

Space exploration is the investigation of outer space using astronomy and space technology. Since the launch of Sputnik 1 in 1957 — the first artificial satellite — humanity has sent probes to every planet in our solar system, walked on the Moon, and built a permanent outpost in orbit: the International Space Station (ISS).

Today, agencies like NASA, ESA, and private companies such as SpaceX are pushing further — with plans to return humans to the Moon and eventually send crewed missions to Mars.

Notable Missions

Apollo 11 — First Moon Landing (1969)

On July 20, 1969, NASA's Apollo 11 successfully landed the first humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin spent over two hours on the lunar surface, while Michael Collins orbited above in the command module. Armstrong's words — "One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind" — echo through history.

Voyager 1 — Into Interstellar Space (1977)

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the most distant human-made object ever built. It crossed the heliopause into interstellar space in 2012 and, as of 2026, is over 24 billion kilometres from Earth — still sending data home.

Quick Facts

  • The Sun is 1.39 million km in diameter — 109 times the width of Earth.
  • Light from the Sun takes approximately 8 minutes 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  • The Milky Way contains over 200 billion stars.
  • Mars hosts Olympus Mons — the tallest volcano in the solar system at 22 km.
  • The ISS travels at ~28,000 km/h and orbits Earth every 90 minutes.
  • There are currently ~10,000 active satellites orbiting Earth.
